Alumni Donor Behavior Analysis – Juniata College

Analyzed five years of alumni athletic donor data to uncover giving patterns across sports, seasons, and engagement categories — with actionable recommendations to reduce mailing costs and improve fundraising targeting.

R Chi-Square Kruskal-Wallis Dunn's Post Hoc Time Series Client Reporting

The Problem

What the Client Needed

Juniata College's development office was spending significant resources mailing fundraising materials to all alumni athletes — regardless of past giving behavior. They needed a data-driven answer to a practical question: who is actually likely to donate, when do they give, and does it depend on which sport they played?

By identifying patterns in engagement and giving, the team could target outreach more precisely, reduce waste, and focus campaign timing around the months that historically drive the most donations.

Each record contained alumni engagement category (Loyal, LYBUNT, SYBUNT, Non-Donor), sport played, fund designated, donation amount, donation date, and fiscal year — spanning both pre- and post-COVID periods.

Methods

Analytical Approach

1

Engagement Category Analysis

Categorized alumni into Loyal (every year), LYBUNT (last year but not this year), SYBUNT (some year but not this year), and Non-Donor segments to understand the retention funnel.

2

Chi-Square Test: Sport vs. Engagement

Tested whether engagement category is independent of sport played using a Chi-Square test of association — determining if certain sports produce systematically more loyal donors.

3

Kruskal-Wallis + Dunn's Post Hoc: Gift Size

Since donation amounts are heavily right-skewed, used non-parametric Kruskal-Wallis to test for differences in gift size across fiscal years, followed by Dunn's test to identify which specific years differed.

4

Seasonal Giving Patterns

Analyzed month-by-month donation counts and totals, separately for pre- and post-COVID periods, to identify the campaign windows with the highest donor activity.

5

Dashboard & Slide Presentation

Compiled findings into a client-facing presentation with visualizations for the development office, translating statistical results into concrete targeting recommendations.

Results

Key Findings

Engagement depends on sport (p < 0.05)

The Chi-Square test confirmed alumni engagement level is statistically dependent on which sport was played — some sports produce systematically more loyal donors than others.

COVID shifted peak giving months

Pre-COVID, December dominated fundraising. Post-COVID, April emerged as a second major peak alongside December — suggesting pandemic-era behavior change in alumni giving calendars.

Golf vs. Women's Soccer: 36x gap in avg gift

Golf alumni averaged $1,489.38 per gift. Women's Soccer alumni averaged $40.87. A 36x difference in average gift size — indicating sport plays a major role in gift magnitude, not just frequency.

2020–2021 was statistically distinct

Dunn's post hoc test identified fiscal year 2020–21 as having significantly different contribution patterns from prior years — a COVID impact visible in the data.

Takeaway

Alumni giving at Juniata College is not uniform — it follows predictable patterns by sport, season, and engagement category. By targeting outreach to high-engagement sports in April and December, and deprioritizing low-engagement segments, the development office can reduce mailing costs and increase campaign ROI without reducing total donations.
